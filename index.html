    📚 Chess Lessons

    How to Play Chess — Video Lesson

    Watch this short guide to learn the rules, how the board is set up, and how each piece moves.

    The Board

    Chess is played on an 8×8 board. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns.

    Ranks are the horizontal rows (1–8). Files are the vertical columns (a–h). The board is set up so each player has a light square on their right.

    White always moves first. The goal is to checkmate your opponent’s king.

    How the Pieces Move

    • King ♔ — One square in any direction. Must never move into check.
    • Queen ♕ — Any number of squares in any direction (rank, file, or diagonal).
    • Rook ♖ — Any number of squares along a rank or file (horizontally or vertically).
    • Bishop ♗ — Any number of squares along a diagonal. Stays on the same color.
    • Knight ♘ — Moves in an “L”: two squares in one direction, then one square perpendicular. It jumps over other pieces.
    • Pawn ♙ — One square forward (or two from the starting row). Captures one square diagonally forward. Does not capture straight ahead.

    Special Moves

    • Castling — Move the king two squares toward a rook, then move that rook to the square the king crossed. You can’t castle out of, through, or into check, or if the king or that rook has already moved.
    • En passant — When a pawn moves two squares and lands beside your pawn, you may capture it “in passing” as if it had moved only one square (on your next move only).
    • Promotion — When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it becomes a queen, rook, bishop, or knight (almost always a queen).

    Check and Checkmate

    Check — Your king is under attack. You must get out of check on your next move (move the king, block, or capture the attacking piece).

    Checkmate — The king is in check and has no legal move to escape. The game ends; the side that delivered checkmate wins.

    Stalemate — It’s your turn, your king is not in check, but you have no legal move. The game is a draw.

    Opening Tips

    • Control the center (e4, d4, e5, d5 and the squares around them).
    • Develop your pieces (knights and bishops) early; avoid moving the same piece too many times.
    • Castle to keep your king safer and connect your rooks.
    • Don’t bring the queen out too early; she can be chased by enemy pieces.
    • Connect your rooks and try to activate them on open files.
